Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is Eagle Ninja?

    Eagle Ninja is a platform for sending prospects and clients on mobile missions (“Quests”) so that they can learn of new features or products, interact and engage with a brand or point of sale, and gamify mobile advertising.

    Mobile Quests can be offline or online, and can take place within the Eagle Ninja native Apps on the App Store and Google Play, or through our Appless Responsive mobile web platform.

  • What is a Quest?

    A Quest is a “Mission” whereby we ask prospects or clients to go to a certain location, or download a certain app, or visit a certain website, view a certain video clip, take a selfie or picture, scan a bar code, play a branded micro game, upload a screenshot, answer questions and similar activities in any type of combination.

  • What kinds of quests are there?

    Quests can be launched within our native apps on the Appstore and Google Play, or by generating a link that launches an apples responsive based Quest.

    Rewards and incentives for Quests can take the form of monetary rewards, promotional coupons, contributions to favourite causes, or digital points.

    Quests can be offline, online or combinations.

    Quests can be accessed from iOS and Android smartphones, as well as via the desktop when using our Appless Responsive Mobile Web Platform

  • Can quests be accssed from the desktop?

    Quests can be accessed from iOS and Android smartphones, as well as via the desktop when using our Appless Responsive Mobile Web Platform

  • How are monetary rewards paid?

    We use PayPal to reward participants of monetary Quests that have been approved.

  • How are promotional coupons delivered

    Quests that are approved and that have a promotional coupon as a reward show the alphanumeric coupon code on a screen once the Quest has been approved, as well as sending it via email to the participant.

  • How are quests approved?

    Each completed Quest is reviewed to verify that the participant successfully completed their “Mission”. Once that review has been completed, which can take up to 24 hours, participants are notified that their Quest has been approved and they can then claim their reward.

  • How long are quests available for?

    The time during which Quests are available can be set to as short as a few hours, or as long as many months.

    In addition, each Quest can have a maximum time available to complete, such that once a Quest has been started or reserved, there are X hours (or days or weeks) for the Quest to be completed, and if it is not completed in that time frame, the Quest “slot” expires and is reassigned for other participants as long as the Quest is still available.

  • How many participants can be invited to a quest?

    As many as you like. You can define X number of “slots” that will be available for participants to start or serve a Quest, and that is the maximum number that will be allowed to start or reserve a Quest.

  • What does reserving a quest mean?

    A participant may wish to conduct a Quest, but at that moment does not have time to start it, so by reserving it they ensure that a Quest slot has been assigned to them, and they can then start it at any time as long as the Quest has not expired (the maximum time allotted to finish the Quest has not been reached).

  • What kind of location, demographic and other triggers can I define in a quest?

    If you use our native apps in the Appstore or Google Play you can set geographic location filters, demographic filters, installed app filters, previously conducted Quest filters and answers to previously conducted Quest filters.

    You can also set geofences for push notifications when entering the geofence.

  • What is the difference between a free quest and a premium quest?

    You can set up, at no cost to you, a free Quest using our Appless Responsive Mobile Web Platform. There are no limits on the number of participants you can invite, availability dates, or Quest duration times.

    Certain features only available in our native apps on the Appstore and Google Play will not be available such as bar and QR code scanning, branded micro games, social sharing of activities during the Quest, demographic and location based filters and similar.

    Also, only promotional coupons (any alphanumeric code) can be used in Free Quests, for other types of rewards such as monetary rewards or contributions to a favourite charity you must set up Premium Quests.

    Validation, attribution and approval of satisfactorily completed Quests is only offered for Premium Quests, in Free Quests all Quests are automatically approved.

  • Do you offer white labelling of your platform?

    Yes. We can set up a private label version of our native apps on Google Play and the Appstore.

    In any event, every Quest you set up is “branded” with your selected background images.

  • Are quests online, offline, or both?

    All of the above, depending on how you define them.

  • Are there limits to the number of activities, or duration, or participants in a free quest?

    No, you can define any availability dates, any duration, any number of activities and any number of participants.

  • Are any types of quests possible?

    We reserve the right, at our sole and entire discretion, to not allow and publish Quests that we consider, at our sole and entire discretion to be offensive or harmful to the public or simply in bad taste.

  • How soon after I define a free quest will they be published?

    We generally take less than 24 hours to review and publish a Free Quest

  • How do I set up a premium quest?

    Please contact us at info@eagleninja.com so we can learn of your objectives, set up a free demo and send you a detailed proposal.

  • Can you set up a demo of a premium quest for free?

    Yes, contact us at info@eagleninja.com so we can learn what kind of demo Premium Quest you would like set up at no charge.
